10. Why do we use media queries?
    We use media queries to make page elements responsive. In other words, media queries tell the browser to change the style of an element, such as photo width or headline font size, at different screen sizes.


11. What is the main difference between a serif and a sans-serif font?
    Serif fonts have feet/small extensions/flourishes (serifs!) at the end of the strokes. Sans serif fonts do not.
    The former are associated with traditional print type, while sans serif are more often used in a clean, modern design aesthetic.
